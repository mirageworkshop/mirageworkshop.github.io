About MiRAGE 2025

About MiRAGE 2025

Effective human-robot collaboration for shared goals is necessary for the seamless integration of robots in human daily lives. To realize such effective human-robot collaborative systems, (1) multimodal scene understanding, (2) robot action planning, and (3) robot manipulation, are essential to provide robots with the capability to interpret their environment, interact with humans based on such understanding, and execute planned actions. This goal lies at the intersection of multiple avenues of research in computer vision, audio, speech, physical sensing/manipulation, and natural language processing. Research on human-robot collaboration technologies has been accelerated to solve issues in different layers divided into various academic communities. The focus of this workshop is to bring together researchers in different fields working on multimodal understanding for modeling human demonstration understanding, robot action planning, and human-robot interaction, to position the current research progress within the goal of achieving human-like machine intelligence capable enough to collaborate with humans, and develop new mechanisms and algorithms to achieve it. MiRAGE 2025 will provide a focused venue for discussing and disseminating research on multimodal understanding to generate robot actions.
